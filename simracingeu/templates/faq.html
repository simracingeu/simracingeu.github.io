    {% trans 'Championship Rules' %}

    {% trans 'What are the types of penalties in the championship?' %}
    The types of penalties defined by their impact on the driver are:
    • {% trans "Minor Penalty:" %} {% trans "Verbal warning or up to 5 seconds added to the result." %}
    • {% trans "Automatic RP Penalty:" %} {% trans "Penalties issued by the Real Penalty system, such as Drive-Through or Stop-and-Go." %}
    • {% trans "Moderate Penalty:" %}
      • {% trans "5-second deduction in the event." %}
      • {% trans "Ballast application in future events." %}
      • {% trans "Deduction of event points." %}
      {% trans "Divided into minor (time only) and major (ballast or points)." %}
    • {% trans "Severe Penalty:" %}
      • {% trans "Event disqualification (no points)." %}
      • {% trans "Suspension from one or more future events." %}
      • {% trans "In serious cases, permanent expulsion from the championship." %}
    • Very Severe Penalty:
      • Expulsion from the championship.
      • Removal from all current and future championships.
    {% trans 'What is considered unsportsmanlike driving?' %}
    {% trans 'Any action that violates championship rules, endangers other drivers, or attempts to gain an unfair advantage is considered unsportsmanlike driving. This may include sudden braking, aggressive blocking, or not respecting blue flags.' %}
    {% trans 'How are on-track incidents handled?' %}
    {% trans 'Any contact with another driver is considered an incident. A ' %}{% trans 'Moderate Penalty' %}{% trans ' will be applied if the contact causes a spin or track departure. Severity is assessed based on speed difference, intent, recurrence, and consequences. Specific criteria are defined for light and moderate contacts in one-on-one situations.' %}
    {% trans 'What rules apply for the start and first laps?' %}
    {% trans 'It is recommended to maintain a safe distance during the first two laps. Maximum caution is advised as incidents in the first lap have greater consequences.' %}
    {% trans 'What are the rules about braking on track?' %}
    {% trans 'Sudden braking on straights is considered a moderate penalty. Early braking as a defensive technique is allowed, with the responsibility on the attacking driver to maintain distance.' %}
    {% trans 'When is returning to the pits allowed?' %}
    {% trans 'Returning to the pits is only allowed when the car is completely unusable or represents a danger. Using it locks the car in the pits. In race sessions, attempting to rejoin after using it results in automatic disqualification. In qualifying, the driver cannot continue and must start the race with their best time before using it. It is allowed if the car is within its assigned pit box.' %}
    {% trans 'What is considered light contact on track?' %}
    {% trans 'In a one-on-one battle, 1 or 2 contacts that don\'t cause a spin or track departure are considered light contacts.' %}
    {% trans 'What is considered moderate contact on track?' %}
    {% trans 'In a one-on-one battle, contact that causes a spin or complete track departure is considered moderate. Three or more incidents without yielding position may result in a severe penalty.' %}
    {% trans 'How are straight-line rear-end collisions classified?' %}
    {% trans 'Straight-line rear-end collisions, which usually occur in braking zones, can be classified as Light or Moderate depending on the consequences, speed difference, loss of control, and position surrender.' %}
    {% trans 'How are side collisions classified?' %}
    {% trans 'Side collisions usually occur from not using radar apps like Helicorsa in a visible screen position. Depending on consequences, speed difference, loss of control, and position surrender, they can be classified as Light or Moderate.' %}
    {% trans 'What rules apply in parallel curves?' %}
    • {% trans 'Front-aligned cars:' %} {% trans 'In a sporting battle, slight trajectory adjustments that allow counter-maneuvers are permitted. Adjustments or positioning that abuse track position may be classified as Moderate.' %}
    • {% trans 'Non-aligned cars (half-car overlap against opponent\'s front):' %} {% trans 'This is considered abusive behavior, not allowing sporting battle, and classified as Moderate.' %}
    • {% trans 'Rear of outside car against front of inside car:' %} {% trans 'Will be reviewed and may be classified as Light/Moderate.' %}
    • {% trans 'Four or more cars aligned in a braking zone:' %} {% trans 'Each driver must respect their "virtual" lane. Leaving this lane to make abusive contact will be classified as Moderate.' %}
    {% trans 'What happens in case of a partial track departure?' %}
    {% trans "If the defending driver loses control by hitting the gravel and the attacking driver (close behind) collides, this may be analyzed as a non-punishable incident due to the defender's loss of control." %}
    {% trans 'What happens in case of a complete track departure?' %}
    {% trans 'It will be reviewed to determine if the attacking driver could have avoided the collision. It may be classified as Light/Moderate.' %}
    {% trans 'What are the rules for overtaking?' %}
    {% trans 'Overtaking is allowed on any side of the track, as long as it doesn\'t interfere with the "racing line" of drivers running parallel. Any driver who hits another during an overtake may be investigated. The severity of the penalty will depend on the speed difference in the collision, consequences for the defending driver, impact on other drivers, and position surrender. Minimal/moderate contact is allowed during position battles if it doesn\'t cause excessive trajectory changes or spins/track departures. When fighting in a braking zone, drivers must maintain a safe distance to avoid collisions from early braking.' %}
    What is considered lane invasion during overtaking?
    In the case of cars aligned in the braking zone, lane invasion is prohibited.
    {% trans 'When is a driver considered to have gained position during an overtake?' %}
    {% trans 'A driver is considered to have gained position (inside/outside) only when their car completely passes the defender. Using Helicorsa and mirrors is recommended to avoid side collisions.' %}
    {% trans 'What are the conditions for overtaking at the start of a curve (braking zone)?' %}
    • {% trans 'Both cars arrive at similar speed/position, with the attacker at least 25% alongside the defender.' %}
    • {% trans 'The attacker\'s trajectory does not obstruct the defender\'s natural lane.' %}
    {% trans 'If these conditions are not met, the attacker must abort the overtake and allow the defender to take their line.' %}
    {% trans 'What distance should drivers maintain between each other?' %}
    {% trans 'When battling (or lapping), drivers must leave enough space. Abruptly closing the gap is classified as Moderate.' %}
    {% trans 'What are the rules for lapped drivers?' %}
    {% trans 'Blue flag rules apply. Lapped drivers must maintain a predictable line and facilitate overtaking by lifting off the throttle. They must yield within a maximum of 4 corners.' %}
    {% trans 'How many direction changes are allowed when defending on a straight?' %}
    {% trans 'When defending on a straight, a driver may make one direction change. Two or more direction changes (e.g., zig-zag) are prohibited and classified as Moderate.' %}
    {% trans 'What should a driver do after a partial track departure?' %}
    {% trans 'A driver who partially leaves the track (3 wheels off) must yield to drivers on track and avoid collisions or obstructions. Not doing so is considered Moderate.' %}
    {% trans 'How is ballast used in the championship?' %}
    {% trans 'Ballast is used to balance performance. If a car demonstrates excessive superiority, organizers may apply ballast (with prior notice).' %}
    {% trans 'What happens if a driver has absences in the championship?' %}
    {% trans 'Drivers are expected to comply with the schedule. Three unjustified absences in a championship may result in exclusion. Events may be canceled if more than 90% of drivers are absent.' %}
    {% trans 'How can protests be submitted?' %}
    {% trans 'Protests must be sent by email to simracingeurope@gmail.com. Public complaints against the organizers or ForoAssetto are prohibited.' %}
    {% trans 'What should a driver do if they lose control of the car?' %}
    {% trans 'A driver who loses control must stop immediately. Partial track departure incidents that cause a drastic loss of speed or direction changes may be penalized.' %}
    {% trans 'What are the rules for pit stops?' %}
    {% trans 'Drivers must not cross the white line at pit entry or exit. RealPenalty applies penalties (e.g., drive-through) for infractions.' %}
    {% trans 'What rules apply to parallel vehicles?' %}
    {% trans 'Vehicles are considered parallel if any part of them overlaps. Each driver must stay in their "imaginary lane", even in scenarios with multiple cars.' %}
    {% trans 'What should a driver do if their car is damaged and immobilized?' %}
    {% trans 'If a car becomes immobilized, the "return to pits" button should be used. Exploiting this function to gain an advantage is considered a Severe infraction.' %}
    {% trans 'What is considered exploitation of rules or the game?' %}
    {% trans 'Deliberate shortcuts on track, using walls to gain speed, or using invalid laps to gain advantage are considered Severe infractions. Examples include skipping chicanes, bouncing off walls to gain speed, or modifying CSP settings.' %}
    {% trans 'How should escape areas be used?' %}
    {% trans 'The use of escape areas must avoid gaining advantages. Repeating this action may be considered Moderate or Severe.' %}
    {% trans 'Can championship rules be changed?' %}
    {% trans 'Yes, these regulations are subject to updates. Version numbers reflect revisions.' %}

    Race Day Procedures

    What time are events scheduled?
    All official SimRacingEU events are scheduled at predefined times. Times will not change unless more than 90% of drivers are absent, in which case the event may be canceled.
    {% trans 'What are the track limits?' %}
    {% trans 'Each circuit has defined its lateral limits, generally marked by solid white lines. Some circuits include curbs and green zones as part of the track. The minimum requirement is that at least one tire remains within the limits at all times. The application of limits is based on Kunos official limits for original circuits, custom limits for modified circuits, and Real Penalty recognition during events.' %}
