Overtaking
Non-lapped drivers
Overtaking is permitted on either side of the track, provided it does not interfere with the "racing line" of side-by-side drivers. Any driver who hits another during an overtake may be investigated. The severity of the penalty will depend on the collision‚Äôs speed differential, consequences for the defending driver, impact on other drivers, and position yielding. Minimal/moderate contact is allowed during position battles if it does not cause excessive trajectory changes or spins/offs. When battling into a braking zone, drivers must maintain a safe distance to avoid collisions from early braking.
Multiple cars in a braking zone: If multiple cars enter a braking zone side-by-side, each driver must respect their "natural lane." Example: If two defending drivers begin braking ahead, an attacking driver behind cannot brake later, switch lanes, and claim a preferred lane. Natural lane* refers to the lane formed between two or more cars during braking and throughout the corner. Neither attacker nor defender may force a driver against the track‚Äôs edges. Each driver must allow sporting battles.
Gaining position: A driver is considered to have gained position (inside/outside) only when their car fully overtakes the defender. Use Helicorsa and mirrors to avoid side collisions.
Conditions for overtaking at the start of a curve (braking zone): Both cars arrive at similar speed/position, with the attacker at least 25% alongside the defender. The attacker‚Äôs trajectory does not obstruct the defender‚Äôs natural lane. If these conditions are not met, the attacker must abort the overtake and allow the defender to take their line. Examples:

Distance from another driver
When battling (or lapping), drivers must leave sufficient space. Abruptly closing space is classified as Moderate.
Lapped drivers:
Blue flag rules apply. Lapped drivers must maintain a predictable line and facilitate overtaking by lifting off the throttle. Lapped drivers must yield within 4 corners.
